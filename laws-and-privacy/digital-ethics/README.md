# Digital Ethics

Surveillance, consent, algorithmic accountability, and the ethics of security.

Ethics in security covers questions that law does not always answer: When is surveillance justified? Who is responsible when automated systems make harmful decisions? What obligations do security researchers have? These questions do not have clean answers, but they have important consequences.
